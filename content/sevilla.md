Our last day and a half in Spain was spent in Sevilla. We went on a bike tour of the city. We biked about 12 km (7.5 miles).

During the tour, we stopped many places to see things. My favorite part was when we were biking along the Guadalquivir river.

Some of things we saw were Plaza de ESPAÑA, a building that looks sort of like a mushroom, the Macarena Church, and lots of huge houses.

Sevilla is the fourth biggest city in Spain. It is a amazing city for biking because it is super flat and the city has the second most bike lanes in Europe.

The tour was amazing and biking was a great way to see the city.

In Sevilla, besides going on a bike tour, we saw an alcazar , and we saw flamenco again. This flamenco was a lot better than the one we saw in Marbella.

– Naomi
